All session long, markets were frozen by one question: would Trump pull the trigger on Iran at his 8 PM ET deadline? Trump posted on Truth Social. Two-week ceasefire. Double-sided. The reaction was immediate: WTI crude oil crashed 16.18% to $94.67 — one of the largest single-day oil price drops in history. Dow futures jumped 967 points. S&P 500 futures surged 2.1%. Why does this matter for the AI thesis? Two words: inflation pressure. The Fed held rates at 3.5–3.75% in March partly because $110+ oil was keeping energy costs and inflation elevated. Oil at $94 and heading lower weakens the Fed's reasoning for staying on hold. Goldman Sachs had pushed their first Fed cut forecast to September because of the oil shock [4]. That timeline may move forward dramatically. Lower rates = higher tech multiples = everything in the AI basket goes up.

The wildcard: this is a 2-week ceasefire, not a peace deal. Iran hasn't officially confirmed in writing as of this session. Gold rose 3.42% — which is unusual, because normally gold falls when geopolitical tension eases. Some traders are still hedging.
