🌍 What's Going On In The World? 世界の状況
// MORNING ALPHA // APR 10 // SOFT CPI + SEMIS RIP + ISLAMABAD TODAY //
Soft core CPI at 2.6% gives bulls the green light — semis ripping +5% on the Broadcom-Google-Anthropic AI chip deal, VIX at 19, and Islamabad talks today could finally resolve the oil premium 😈

The week’s most important data point just landed and it’s bullish. Core CPI came in at 2.6% YoY, 0.1 percentage points below consensus. Headline CPI was 3.3%, driven almost entirely by a record 23% monthly spike in gasoline prices from the Hormuz disruption [1]. The critical read: the energy shock is supply-driven, not demand-driven. Core inflation is actually decelerating. This gives the Fed intellectual cover to maintain its one-cut guidance, and the market is treating it as a green light for growth assets.

The semiconductor complex is having its best session of the week. AMD +5.13% to $248.77, AVGO +5.19% to $373.32, TSM +3.20%, ASML +2.98%, NVDA +1.66% [2]. Three converging catalysts are driving the move: first, Broadcom confirmed a three-way deal with Google and Anthropic to supply custom TPU-based AI chips — Anthropic simultaneously crossed a $30B annualized run rate. This validates the entire custom silicon thesis and signals hyperscaler AI capex is not slowing. Second, the soft core CPI removed the rate-fear overhang that had compressed multiples since mid-March. Third, this is a technical V-recovery from severely oversold levels (AVGO bounced from $290 to $373 in 10 sessions) [3].

The Islamabad diplomatic talks are the second major catalyst today. VP Vance is personally leading the US delegation — an unusual level of commitment that signals Washington wants a deal. Iran submitted a 10-point proposal covering the nuclear program, sanctions relief, and regional security [4]. Even a modest framework agreement that reopens partial Hormuz transit could send crude from $98 back toward the low $80s. Oil at $98.49 (+4.32%) has a massive geopolitical premium baked in; the Opus council argues the smart trade is fading the oil bounce via USO puts with Islamabad as the catalyst [2].

The risk flag the market is ignoring: second-order energy inflation passthrough. Gas surged 23% in a single month — the highest monthly spike on record for the gasoline index. This hasn’t hit airfares, shipping costs, food production, or petrochemical inputs yet. Even if Hormuz partially reopens, the March energy shock is already embedded in the supply chain and will surface in April–June CPI prints [1]. Today’s clean core print is not a permanent state — the June and July CPI reports will be the real verdict.

APLD bounced +8.94% to $27.86 as post-earnings dip buyers stepped in — the 2x revenue beat ($161.8M vs $75.5M estimate) is reasserting itself over the GAAP miss noise. XOM fell -0.97% to $153.53 despite oil at $98, a meaningful divergence that suggests energy equities are already pricing in Islamabad progress. The week’s setup heading into the weekend: soft CPI + Islamabad potential = the clearest path to a sustained rally. Hot passthrough CPI in June + Islamabad failure = the downside scenario the market isn’t pricing [2]
