Day two of the ceasefire: WTI posting its biggest crash in years at -17.5%, VIX collapsed -19%, and the AI/semiconductor complex is ripping — but the ceasefire expires April 17 and a GDP + CPI data minefield starts tomorrow 😈

The US–Iran ceasefire, announced at 8 PM ET Tuesday, is now producing one of the largest single-day oil crashes in modern trading history. WTI crude fell $19.78 to $93.17 (-17.51%) [1] as the war premium baked in since Iran threatened the Strait of Hormuz evaporated overnight. Brent crude dropped $13.79 to $95.48 per barrel [2]. This is the macro domino every risk-on trader was waiting for.

Equity markets responded with a broad-based relief rally. The S&P 500 is up +2.28% to 6,767.56, Nasdaq +2.89% to 22,653, Dow +2.71% [3]. The VIX collapsed from ~26 to 20.88 (-19.01%). The mechanism: lower oil → lower inflation → earlier Fed rate cuts → higher growth multiples. Every AI and semiconductor name is catching the second-order tailwind.

The semiconductor complex is leading. MU +7.19% to $404.71 as AI memory demand fires alongside receding inflation pressure. AEHR Test Systems surged +24.62% on a Q3 earnings beat — semiconductor test equipment is a high-beta proxy for the chip capex cycle revival. SOXL ripped +16.94%. Today’s earnings catalyst: Delta Air Lines (DAL) reports Q1 2026 at 10 AM ET [4]. All three LLM Council members (Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4) flagged DAL as the #1 same-day expression of this oil crash — airlines are the most direct beneficiaries and Delta’s Trainer refinery provides additional structural upside.

The LLM Council is unanimous on the key risk flag: the market is pricing “ceasefire = permanent peace” when this expires April 17 and Iran’s core demands remain unresolved. Gold is up +2.56% to $4,804 on a day when fear should be receding — institutional players are quietly hedging. Crypto is flat (BTC -0.31%, ETH -0.03%) when it should be ripping on a genuine risk-on event. The data minefield opens tomorrow: GDP + PCE (Apr 9), CPI (Apr 10). A hot print could unwind this entire rally instantly.

The notable outlier: PLTR is down -0.44% while the Nasdaq rips +2.89%. When a high-multiple growth name lags a broad rally of this magnitude, it’s information — the defense/gov-AI premium that lifted PLTR is deflating as the ceasefire narrative takes hold. This is not a sell signal, but a watch: concentration risk at ~85% PLTR deserves attention when the thesis name lags the index by 330 basis points on a monster green day
